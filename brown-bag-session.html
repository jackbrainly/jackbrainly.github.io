25. Explain the difference of the JAD Session to Interview session.


Answer:

JAD (Joint Application Development) sessions are a type of meeting attended by members of a project team, stakeholders, and sometimes customers, that uses a structured process to develop and implement a solution to a problem. The primary objective of a JAD session is to gain consensus on a solution to a problem. The session is typically lead by a facilitator who guides the discussion and ensures that all participants are heard.

In contrast, an interview session is a formal meeting between a potential employee and an employer. Interviews are used to assess the qualifications, experience, and skills of a candidate in order to determine if they are a good fit for the position. Interviews are typically more structured than JAD sessions, and the interviewer will ask a series of predetermined questions of the candidate in order to determine if they have the necessary skills and experience for the role.
