ACTIVITY I Following Plato's claim that "specialization is the reason for and justification of society," do you think the majority of workers/employees/officials listed below are distributed according to their specialization (or that they belong to the right field)? Defend your answer. a, government officials (elected) b. government employees c. teachers (public schools) d. weather forecasters e. party-list representatives in the Lower House of Congress​


Explanation:

a. Government officials (elected) - It depends on the specific government officials being considered. Some elected officials may have specialized in a certain field prior to their election and therefore may be well-suited to their roles. For example, an elected official with a background in law may be well-suited to serve as a judge or attorney general. However, some elected officials may not have a specialization that directly relates to their role and may not be as well-suited to their position.

b. Government employees - Similar to government officials, government employees may or may not have specialized in a field related to their role. Some government employees may have specialized in fields such as finance or information technology, which are directly relevant to their role. Others may not have a specialized field of expertise, but may have acquired skills and experience that are relevant to their role.

c. Teachers (public schools) - Teachers are expected to have specialized in their subject matter and in the teaching profession. However, in some cases, they may not have a specific specialization relevant to their subject matter but they are still qualified to teach by virtue of obtaining a degree in Education.

d. Weather forecasters - Weather forecasters are expected to have specialized in fields such as meteorology and atmospheric science, in order to accurately predict and report on weather patterns.

e. Party-list representatives in the Lower House of Congress - Party-list representatives are elected based on the platforms and advocacies of their respective party-list groups. They may have specialized in fields such as law, education, health, labor, and the environment, which align with the agendas of their party-list groups. However, not all party-list representatives have specialized fields, but they may have acquired relevant experiences and knowledge in their advocacies.

In summary, while some workers may be distributed according to their specialization, others may not have a direct specialization but still possess relevant skills and experiences that allow them to effectively perform their roles.

Working Equation: x + x - 129 = 297
