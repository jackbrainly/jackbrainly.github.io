2. NDRRMC and PDRRMC means?

3. ano ang tanggapan ng PDRRMC​


Ang Pambansang Tanggapan para sa Pagtugon ng Sakuna o National Disaster Risk Reduction & Management Council (NDRRMC) na dating kilala bilang National Disaster Coordinating Council (NDCC) ay isang sangay ng pamahalaan ng Pilipinas sa ilalim ng Kagawaran ng Tanggulang Pambansa na may pananagutan na tiyakin ang kaligtasan at kabutihan ng mga mamamayan sa panahon ng sakuna.

4. ano Ang kaibahan Ng PDRRMC at CBDRRM​


Ang PDRRMC ay kumakatawan sa Provincial Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Council. Ang pangunahing layunin ng PDRRMC ay upang bumuo at magpatupad ng isang komprehensibo at pinagsamang plano sa pagbabawas ng peligro sa sakuna at maging responsable para sa koordinasyon at pagpapatupad ng pamamahala ng kalamidad sa buong mga yugto ng pagpaplano, pangyayari at pagbawi.

Ang CBDRM o tinatawag na Community Based Disaster Risk Management, ito ang sangay na nagmomonitor sa iba't ibang komunidad sa tuwing magkakaroon ng sakuna. Sa tulong ng CBDRM, nababawasan ang mga casualties sa isang sakuna. Naagapan ang anumang pinsalang madudulot ng mga sakuna. Nabibigyan din ng agarang tulong ang mga biktima ng kalamidad o sakuna.

14. the PDRRMC advise the residents living within the 10 km radius critical area to evacuate due to eminent eruption of a volcano.On the map that is drawn on a coordinate plane,the coordinates corresponding to the location of the volcano is(3,4).what is the equation of the circle (general form)enclosing the general area​


Answer:

[tex]x^{2} +y^{2} -6x -8y -75 = 0[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

For this problem, let us assume that each unit in the coordinate plane corresponds to 1 km.

Circles in the coordinate plane may have 2 forms, the general form, or the center-radius form.

The general form of a circle is given by the form

[tex]x^{2} + y^{2} + Cx + Dy + E = 0[/tex]

Where C, D and E are constants, or any real numbers.

The equation of a circle in center-radius form is

[tex](x-h)^{2} + (y-k)^{2} = r^{2}[/tex]

where the center is at the point [tex](h,k)[/tex] and the radius of the circle is [tex]r[/tex].

Both forms appear as a circle on the coordinate plane. Either can be used but the center-radius form is used more often due to convenience. The coordinates of a center and the radius are both important to a circle that is why the center-radius form exists.

To convert the equation of a circle from general form to center-radius form, we complete the square to find the center. To convert the equation of a circle from center-radius form to general form, we expand the expressions and transfer the radius to the left side.

Since we are given the radius of a circle, and the coordinates of the center, we can easily put it in the equation of center-radius form.

The center of the circle, or the volcano is at [tex](3,4)[/tex] and the radius is 10.

Substituting to the center-radius form gives us:

[tex](x-3)^{2} + (y-4)^{2} = 10^{2}[/tex]

We can evaluate and expand the expression to get the general form:

[tex]x^{2} - 6x + 9 +y^{2}-8y+16=100\\\\x^{2} +y^{2} -6x -8y +9+16-100 = 0\\\\x^{2} +y^{2} -6x -8y +25-100=0\\\\x^{2} +y^{2} -6x -8y -75 = 0[/tex]

The equation of the circle that encloses the area is [tex]x^{2} +y^{2} -6x -8y -75 = 0[/tex].
