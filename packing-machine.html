If 3 machines can pack 200,000 cans in 2 hours, how many machines are needed to pack the same number of cans in 3/4 hours?

Create an equation base on the problemList down the important values or the givensSolve for the unknown variable of the equation you createdVerify your answer

Given: 3 machines can pack 200,000 cans in 2hrs

Equation: Work = Number x Rate x Time

Base on the equation:

Let work be the cans number equals 3 machinestime equals 2 hours

====================================

To solve the unknown value.

First lets assume that the machines have thesame rate.So we will get their rate first

Finding the rate:

Work = Number x Rate x Time

200,000 = 3 x Rate x 2

200,000 = 6Rate

33,333 = Rate

26. Jun has 5 packs of chicken weighin 1 ⅖ kg per pack. He repacked the chicken such that each pack just weighs ½ kg. How many new packen would he be able to make out of the original 5 packs?​ A. 11 packs B. 12 packs C. 13 packs D. 14 packs


Answer:

D. 14 packs

Step-by-step explanation:

First, multiply the original number of packs by the weight of each pack to get the total weight of chicken.

[tex] \\ \sf{} = 5 \times 1 \frac{2}{5} [/tex]

[tex]\\ \sf{} = 5 \times \frac{1 \times 5 + 2}{5} [/tex]

[tex]\\ \sf{} = 5 \times \frac{7}{5} [/tex]

[tex]\\ \sf{} = \frac{5 \times 7}{5} [/tex]

[tex]\\ \sf{} = \frac{35}{5} [/tex]

[tex]\\ \sf{} = 7[/tex]

There are 7 kilograms of chicken, repacking it by ½ kilogram each pack,

[tex]\\ \sf{} = 7 \div \frac{1}{2} [/tex]

[tex]\\ \sf{} = 7 \times \frac{2}{1} [/tex]

[tex]\\ \sf{} = \frac{7 \times 2}{1} [/tex]

[tex]\\ \sf{} = \frac{14}{1} [/tex]

[tex]\\ \large\boxed {\sf{} = 14}[/tex]

We have now 14 packs of chicken.
