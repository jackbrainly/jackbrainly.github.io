6. 2. A solution is made containing 14.6 g CH2OH in 184 g H20. Calculate(a) the mole fraction of CH3OH.(b) the mass percent of CH3OH,(c) the molality of CH3OH.​


Given:

mass of solute = 14.6 g

mass of solvent = 184 g

solute: CH₃OH

solvent: H₂O

Required:

mole fraction of solute

mass percent

molality

Solution: (a)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of each component.

For solute

molar mass of solute = (12.01 g/mol × 1) + (1.008 g/mol × 4) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solute = 32.042 g/mol

For solvent

molar mass of solvent = (1.008 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solvent = 18.016 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each component.

For solute

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{14.6 g}}{\text{32.042 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 0.45565196 mol}[/tex]

For solvent

[tex]\text{moles of solvent} = \frac{\text{mass of solvent}}{\text{molar mass of solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solvent} = \frac{\text{184 g}}{\text{18.016 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solvent = 10.2131439 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mole fraction of solute.

[tex]\text{mole fraction of solute} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{moles of solute + moles of solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mole fraction of solute} = \frac{\text{0.45565196 mol}}{\text{0.45565196 mol + 10.2131439 mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mole fraction of solute} = 0.0427}[/tex]

Solution: (b)

Step 1: Calculate the mass percent of solute by direct substitution.

[tex]\text{mass percent} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solute + mass of solvent}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass percent} = \frac{\text{14.6 g}}{\text{14.6 g + 184 g}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass percent} = 7.35\%}[/tex]

Solution: (c)

Step 1: Calculate the molality of solution by using direct substitution.

Since the moles of solute is already obtained in Step 2 for solving the mole fraction of solute, which is 0.45565196 mol, and the mass of solvent is already given, which is 184 g or 0.184 kg, the molality of the solution is

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent (kg)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{0.45565196 mol}}{\text{0.184 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molality} = 2.48 \: m}[/tex]
