23. examples of seed orchard


A seed orchard is a plantation of trees isolated to reduce pollination from other species of trees and produce genetically the same type of organism. It is intensively managed to produce frequent, abundant, easily harvested seed crops. It is established by setting out clones (as grafts or cuttings) or seedling progeny of trees selected for desired characteristics.


Types of Seed orchard:

1. Clonal Seed Orchards

Clonal seed orchards are constituted by vegetative materials such as grafts, cuttings or plantlets derived from tissue culture are planted in an area of isolation under favorable conditions suitable for fertilization, flowering and ample production of seeds.


2. Seedling Seed Orchards

The progeny, or the offspring are planted at normal spacing . The identity of families is maintained in order to allow for thinning among families and individuals within families based on their phenotypic performance.
