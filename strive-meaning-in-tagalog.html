16. what do we mean when we say that the visual arts strive for the total sensory experience?


Answer:

Art involves the experience of the artist, who is trying to capture something he has seen or felt. It also involves the experience of the viewer, who shares what the artist is attempting to convey.

Explanation:

At its heart, art is about communication. It doesn’t have to be clear, precise, or even relevant; it just has to be something the artist intends to convey, even if that communication is “look at how pretty this is.”

Experience in art means two things to me: events of life that influence how and what you want to say; time spent making art that influence your level of mastery in communicating. In the first part, experience is about the things you’ve done, seen, and felt and have given some thought to expressing. These are the experiences that give art more power to communicate. For the second part, skills are developed not only to manipulate your tools as an artist, but to make them part of your expressive processes, and that usually takes repetition and effort over time.

30. What does the statement striving for success without hard work is like trying to harvest where you planted mean ?


...

The statement "striving for success without hard work is like trying to harvest where you planted" means that in order to achieve success, one must put in effort and work hard. The analogy of trying to harvest where you planted refers to the fact that in agriculture, one cannot expect to reap a harvest without putting in the necessary work to cultivate the crops, such as planting, watering, weeding, and harvesting. Similarly, in life, if one wants to achieve success, they must put in the necessary effort, dedication, and hard work to achieve their goals. Simply hoping for success without taking action or putting in the necessary work is unlikely to yield positive results.
