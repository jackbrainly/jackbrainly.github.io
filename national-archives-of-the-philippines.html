4. How can you promote and preserve Philippine Archives?​


The National Archives of the Philippines houses around 60 million papers spanning centuries of Spanish rule, American and Japanese occupations, and the Republic's early years. It is also the country's final depository for voluminous notarized documents.

The Americans established the Office of Archives (previously the Spanish Division of Archives) under the Treaty of Paris (December 10, 1898). The Treaty called on Spain and the Philippines to hand up their documents. The Bureau of Archives was founded under the Department of Public Instructions by Philippine Commission Act No. 273 (October 21, 1901). (now Department of Education)

The National Archives of the Philippines was established on May 21, 2007, by virtue of RA 9470, the first archival law in more than a century. The new law strengthened the government's recordkeeping systems and archival materials administration operations.

Today, the Archives takes a two-pronged approach to fulfilling its mission, combining excellent records administration with the promotion of a sense of pride in one's past.

Mission : To enhance transparency, public accountability and responsible governance, promote freedom of information, provide access to official records, preserve and popularize Filipino cultural heritage, and strengthen national identities while building international understanding through the implementation of programs on records management and archives administration.

22. what is internet archive?


Answer:

The Internet Archive is an American digital library with the stated mission of "universal access to all knowledge". It provides free public access to collections of digitized materials, including websites, software applications/games, music, movies/videos, moving images, and millions of books

Explanation:

The Internet Archive, a 501(c)(3) non-profit, is building a digital library of Internet sites and other cultural artifacts in digital form. Like a paper library, we provide free access to researchers, historians, scholars, the print disabled, and the general public. Our mission is to provide Universal Access to All Knowledge.

They began in 1996 by archiving the Internet itself, a medium that was just beginning to grow in use. Like newspapers, the content published on the web was ephemeral - but unlike newspapers, no one was saving it.
