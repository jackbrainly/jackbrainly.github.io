16. A farmer can plow a field in 4 days by using a tractor. A hired hand can plow the same field in 6 days by using a smaller tractor. How many days will be required for the plowing if they work together?​


Answer:

2.4 Days

Step-by-step explanation:

A farmer can plow a field in 4 days by using a tractor. A hired hand can plow the same field in 6 days by using a smaller tractor. How many days will be required for the plowing if they work together?

Let x be the number of days required if they work together.

[tex] \frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{1}{x} [/tex]

__________________

LCD is 12.

[tex] = \frac{12 \times 1}{4} + \frac{12 \times 1}{6} \\ = \frac{12}{4} + \frac{12}{6} \\ = 3 + 2 \\ = 5[/tex]

_______________________

[tex] \frac{5}{12} = \frac{1}{x} \\ \\ \frac{5}{12} x = 1 \\ \\ \frac{5x}{12} = 1 \\ \\ 5x = 1 \times 12 \\ \\ 5x = 12 \\ \\ \frac{5x}{5} = \frac{12}{5} \\ \\ x = \frac{12}{5} \\ \\ x = 2.4[/tex]

_____________

2.4 Days is required for the plowing if they work together.

Explanation:

CARABAO

PROS:  

• the manure can be used as fertilizer

• cheaper

• no need to use gas

• helps with the additional labor of tilling the land

• makes a good company

CONS:

• slow when used for transportation

• useless when it dies from an animal disease

• if tired or sick, cannot be used

• cannot easily rotavate the field

• Carabao cannot finish land preparation on time/t

TRACTOR

PROS:

• Carabao cannot finish land preparation on time

• Better quality land preparation with tractor especially on weedy fields

• Rotavation possible only with tractor

• Stronger in harrowing

• Reduces labor

CONS:

• Expensive

• Needs more money for maintenance and gas
