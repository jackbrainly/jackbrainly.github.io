Why carabao is the national animal of the Philippines?

Known as the Pinoy farmer's best buddy in the rice field, a carabao, or kalabaw in Tagalog, is a domestic swamp-type water buffalo (Bubalus bubalis) endemic to the Philippines. Considered the national animal of the Philippines, the carabao represents the Filipinos' hard work and perseverance.

A big part would be because we are a 3rd world country. Yes, Filipinos are seen as hardworking people. A great example would be OFWs. That is the biggest reason why Filipinos are easily hired as OFWs. However, we cannot deny that the Philippines ' currency and cost of living are relatively low compared to other neighboring Southeast Asian countries, which in turn matches the salaries of our workers, in any field, as quite low when compared to other countries. This is essentially causing "Brain Drain."

Brain drain is a slang term indicating substantial emigration or migration of individuals. Brain drain is a massive part of why the Philippines is struggling to develop. Specialists and experts in any field are working abroad instead of staying and working in our country. The lack of proper living wages that can support families is the biggest reason why people go abroad for work. (An example would be engineers. Engineers may get salaries from 11,000 pesos up to 79,600 pesos if they work in the Philippines. However, if Filpino engineers work abroad, they can easily get salaries ranging from 100,000 pesos and above.)

Another part would be the undeniable, rampant corruption issue in the Philippines. Money that could've developed our country for a better future is merely pocketed by certain officials. Money that could help solve our lack of jobs that can give living wages to workers that are supporting their own families.

Answer:

The Philippine Carabao Center (Filipino: Sentro ng Kalabaw sa Pilipinas[2] or Sentro ng Pilipinas para sa Kalabaw[3]) an attached agency of the Department of Agriculture, was established at Science City of Muñoz in Nueva Ecija province in 1992 to breed and cross carabao based on high-yield Murrah buffalo (native breed of Haryana state of India) in the Philippines as a multi-purpose animal that can be raised for milk, meat, hide, and draft.
