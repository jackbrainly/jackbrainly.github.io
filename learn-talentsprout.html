The pupils will learn to avoid junk foods after their symposum last week.

As a modal auxiliary verb, will is particularly versatile, having several different functions and meanings. It is used to form future tenses, to express willingness or ability, to make requests or offers, to complete conditional sentences, to express likelihood in the immediate present, or to issue commands.

When you're talking about the future, “will” is a very common and simple way of describing what you anticipate or plan to happen.

For example, “She will be catching the later train, but I will travel afterwards.”

Answer:
The pupils learned to avoid junk foods after their symposium last week.

8. verbal sentence with the word learning, to learn, and learned


Answer:

LEARNING

Learning sentence example. I started learning to shoot a laser gun today, she said. We never will have the opportunity to learn from the details of their lives and the trillions upon trillions of trial-and-error learning that humankind has repeated again and again. ... He was also a man of learning and culture.

LEARNED

Use either learned or learned in past simple positive sentences: The children learned about squirrels in school yesterday. I learned to play the piano at the age of five.

Future Perfect: She will have learned everything by the end of the month.

Future Possibility: She might learn something new.

Real Conditional: If she learns Russian, she will travel to Moscow.

Unreal Conditional: If she learned Russian, she would travel to Moscow.

Past Unreal Conditional: If she had learned Russian, she would have traveled to Moscow.

Present Modal: She can learn easily.

Past Simple Passive: Poker was learned quickly by all.

Past Continuous: She was learning her lesson when he telephoned.

Past Continuous Passive: The lesson was being learned when he arrived.

Past Perfect: She had learned the song by heart before Jack did.

Past Perfect Passive: The song had learned by heart by the class before the singer arrived.

Past Perfect Continuous: Our children had been learning English for two months before we moved.
