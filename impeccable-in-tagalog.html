8. Although he ------------ his record of success, his failure to bring in any new business told another story. A. clamored ... mediocre B. touted ... impeccable C. vilified... outstanding D. minimized... humble proclaimed ... modest E.


CONTEXT CLUES

A statement itself, the text immediately before or after it, or both can contain contextual information. Because we learn the majority of our vocabulary through reading, we must be able to recognize and use context cues.

Although he CLAMORED his record of success, his MEDIOCRE failure to bring in any new business told another story.

Here are the supporting details:

The clamor of the multitude at the gates; a loud noise, as from a crowd of people. A loud declaration of desire or dissatisfaction: the clamor of the law's supporters. The senators were unable to ignore the public uproar against additional taxes. Synonyms of clamor are loud crying; uproar, cry out, cause a scene.

There are many things in life that are ultimately up to you to decide. Being mediocre indicates that you have made the decision to stay and work inside your comfort zone. Being average also entails choosing to lead a dull life and extolling your present/past circumstances as the reason for it.

When you describe something as mediocre, you are implying that you believe it may be better despite its average quality. His academic performance was average.

Additional information:

Examples of context clues include synonyms, antonyms, word structure suggestions, parallels (including metaphors and similes), and contrasts. For instance, words with the same meaning that are close by are shown as context recommendations for synonyms.

For instance, in a story about cats, the reader will expect to see vocabulary related to cats. These are indications at meaning or semantics.

Syntactic indicators suggest that the placement of words inside a phrase can indicate the part of speech that a missing word must belong to (for example, a verb).

Picture cues: From an early age, beginning readers are trained to look at the drawings to help them recognize a word.
