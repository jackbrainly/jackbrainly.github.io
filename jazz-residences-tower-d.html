Answer:

Jazz Chant

Jazz chants are a unique way of linking the rhythm of natural spoken language to the rhythm of music. Jazz chants were first created by Carolyn Graham, undisputed queen of the genre, over 30 years ago. Carolyn was teaching ESL at New York University by day and working as a singer/piano player in bars at night when, as she has said, she ‘stumbled upon’ the brilliant idea of connecting the natural rhythms of spoken American English with the rhythms of traditional American jazz music in order to help her students develop speaking skills.

29. early jazz was entirely A.improvised B.sung C. european D. african


Early jazz was entirely A.improvised B.sung C. european D. african

Jazz, mainly musical improvisation, created by the African Americans  And influenced by both the harmonic European structure and African rhythms.  It was partly developed from ragtime and blues and is mostly distinguished by syncopated rhythms, polyphonic  ensemble playing, varying degrees of improvisation, mostly intentional pitch variations, and the use of original timbres.

For example , early attempts to describe jazz as a music whose chief characteristic was improvisation turned out to be  too limiting and essentially untrue, since composition, arrangement, and ensemble were all important components of jazz for much of its existence.  Likewise, in fact, syncopation and swing, often considered important and unique to  jazz, lack most authentic jazz, be it from the 1920s or later decades.
