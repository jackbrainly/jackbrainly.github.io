Answer:

1.BORDER LINE

1. A line that establishes or marks a border.

2. An indefinite area intermediate between two qualities or conditions: The borderline between love and hate is often thin

2.ORTHOGRAPHIC PROJECTION

Orthographic projection is a means of representing three-dimensional objects in two dimensions. It is a form of parallel projection, in which all the projection lines are orthogonal to the projection plane, resulting in every plane of the scene appearing in affine transformation on the viewing surface.

3.ISOMETRIC DRAWING

the representation of an object on a single plane (as a sheet of paper) with the object placed as in isometric projection but disregarding the foreshortening of the edges parallel to the three principal axes of the typical rectangular solid, lines parallel to these axes appearing in their true lengths and producing an appearance of distortion

4.LEADER LINE

A leader line is a line referring to some form of feature that could be a dimension, an object or an outline. A leader line consists of two parts. These are:  A type B line (thin, continuous, straight) going from the instruction to the feature.
