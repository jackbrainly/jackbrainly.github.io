4. what are the different safety measures in horticultural crop productionneed asap:^​*junior high school


Here are some safety measures in horticultural crop production:

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Use PPE such as gloves, safety glasses, and dust masks to protect against pesticides and other harmful chemicals.

Proper Storage of Chemicals: Ensure that chemicals are stored in a safe place and away from food, water, and animals.

Safe Use of Machinery: Follow manufacturer's instructions and safety guidelines when operating machinery such as tractors, tillers, and other equipment.

Proper Use and Handling of Chemicals: Follow the instructions on the chemical label and use protective gear when handling and applying chemicals.

Training and Education: Educate workers on safe handling of chemicals and machinery to reduce the risk of accidents.

Emergency Preparedness: Have an emergency plan in place in case of accidental exposure to chemicals or other hazards.

Regular Maintenance: Ensure that machinery is well-maintained and in good working condition to reduce the risk of accidents.

Proper Disposal: Properly dispose of used chemicals and containers to prevent contamination of soil and water.

Temperature is a primary factor affecting the

rate of plant development. Warmer

temperatures expected with climate change

and the potential for more extreme

temperature events will impact plant

productivity. Pollination is one of the most

sensitive phenological stages to temperature

extremes across all species and during this

developmental stage temperature extremes

would greatly affect production. Few

adaptation strategies are available to cope with

temperature extremes at this developmental

stage other than to select for plants which shed

pollen during the cooler periods of the day or

are indeterminate so flowering occurs over a
