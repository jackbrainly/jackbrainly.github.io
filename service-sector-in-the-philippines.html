18. Explain how goods and services; and payments flow and passes from one sector to another sector in the economy.


Answer:

Goods and services flow from producers to consumers in an economy. Producers create goods and services and sell them to consumers, who in turn use those goods and services. Payment for goods and services flows in the opposite direction, from consumers to producers.

In order for goods and services to flow from one sector to another, payments must also flow. For example, a farmer may produce goods (such as crops) and sell them to a grocer, who then sells them to consumers. In this case, payment flows from the consumers to the grocer, then to the farmer.

Payments can flow through intermediaries, such as banks and financial institutions, which facilitate transactions by providing services such as lending, currency exchange, and electronic payments.

Additionally, government plays a role in the economy by providing goods and services (such as education and healthcare) and collecting taxes, which are used to fund these services. Payments for government goods and services flow from taxpayers to the government.

Overall, goods and services flow in one direction, and payments flow in the opposite direction, through various intermediaries and sectors of the economy

Explain how goods and services; and payments flow and passes from one sector to another sector in the economy.

Goods and services flow and payments pass from one sector to another sector in the economy through a series of transactions. When a consumer purchases a good or service from a business, the consumer pays the business for the item. This payment then flows from the consumer to the business, and the business uses this money to purchase the necessary materials and services from other businesses or suppliers in order to produce the good or service. This creates a flow of payments from businesses to suppliers, which then flow back to businesses and consumers as the goods and services are produced and sold. This cycle of production, consumption, and payments then continues as the goods and services are bought and sold in the economy, creating a perpetual flow of payments from one sector to the next.

25. why do we call the service sector as the intermediary unit to the other sector​


We call the service sector the intermediary sector because it provides services to customers related to the production and trade sector.

For example, a service company offers services in the field of cleaning or security. Of course, in providing services and business activities they need a supply of hygiene items such as cleaning soap, gloves, and cleaning machines, and a supply of security items such as uniforms, weapons, and others. And then, they will get the supply from producers or trading sector companies that sell these goods. So producers and goods companies do not sell their goods directly to consumers but through intermediaries from service provider companies. That's why the service sector is called the intermediary sector.
