12. plaza libertadano ang tungkol sa plaza libertad ​


Answer:

Plaza Libertad is a historic plaza located in the heart of Iloilo City Proper, the downtown district, at the southeastern end of Calle Real. It is where the flag of the first Philippine Republic was raised in triumph after Spain surrendered Iloilo, her last capital in the Philippines, to the revolutionary forces led by Gen. Martin Delgado on December 25, 1898.

It is surrounded of many of the city’s famous landmarks including San Jose  Church, Iloilo City Hall, the Masonic Temple, and the Lacson clan ancestral house.

13. The stretch of Manila de Bay where MOA now stands used to be a coastal area with some patches of mangroves. What are the impacts of land developments in this area? Why? ​


Answer:

The area would be prone to flooding and an increased vulnerability to damages induced by tsunamis and increased sea levels.

Explanation:

Mangroves play vital roles in serving as natural buffers against tsunamis, storm surges, and flooding. Patches of mangroves can strengthen the seafloor by providing support from its roots thus the area would not be prone to erosion during shifting tides creating a barrier against the sudden increase in sea level and flooding. In addition to this, mangroves are able to utilize large amounts of carbon dioxide from the air which aids in air pollution. Lastly, mangroves are natural nurseries for some species of fish and could therefore aid in the repopulation of our seas.
