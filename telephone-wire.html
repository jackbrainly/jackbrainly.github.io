6. An 8-meter telephone pole is not standing upstraight. There is a wire attached to the top of thepole and anchored in the ground forming anacute triangle. The wire is 8.5 meters long. Thewire makes a 65° angle with the ground. Whatangle does the telephone pole make with theground?​


LAW OF SINES AND COSINES

The angle the telephone pole makes with the ground is 74°.

FURTHER EXPLANATION

We can determine the length of a triangle's side or the size of one of its angles using the principles of sine and cosine. The law of sines or the law of cosines can be used, depending on the facts at hand. The triangle's length of two sides is related to their intermediate angle by the law of cosines, and the length of one side is related to the sine of its angle by the law of sines.

What is the formula for the law of sines?

The law of the sines formula is an equation that connects the sines of each angle's corresponding side in a triangle. The equation for the law of sines is as follows:

[tex]\boxed{\frac{a}{\sin A}=\frac{b}{\sin B}=\frac{c}{\sin C}}[/tex]

where A, B, and C stand for the triangle's angles while a, b, and c stand for the lengths of the triangle's sides. The angles signify the sides on either side. This indicates that, as we can see in the following triangle (See Fig 1.), and is the side opposite angle A, b is the side opposite angle B, and c is the side opposite angle C.

What is the formula for the law of cosines?

A mathematical equation that connects the lengths of two sides of a triangle to their angle is known as the law of cosines. The law of cosines has the following formula:

[tex]a^2=b^2+c^2 -2bc \cos A\\[/tex][tex]b^2 = a^2 + c^2 -2ac\cos B[/tex][tex]c^2=a^2+b^2-2ab\cos C[/tex]

Thus, as illustrated in the following graphic (See Fig 1.), A, B, and C are the angles of the triangle, and a, b, and c are the lengths of the triangle's sides.

An 8-meter telephone pole is not standing up straight. There is a wire attached to the top of the pole and anchored in the ground forming an acute triangle. The wire is 8.5 meters long. The wire makes a 65° angle with the ground.

Question

What angle does the telephone pole make with the ground?​

Solution

See Fig 2. Let's say AB is the pole and AC is the wire

AB = 8 AC = 8.5∠C = 65°

We will determine ∠B with the law of sines.

[tex]\boxed{\frac{8}{\sin 65}=\frac{8.5}{\sin B}}[/tex]

Then

8 · sin B = (8.5) · sin 65 sin B = [tex]\frac{8.5}{8}[/tex]·  sin 65 B = arc sin ([tex]\frac{8.5}{8}[/tex]﻿·  sin 65) B = 74°

Therefore, the angle the telephone pole makes with the ground is 74°.

17. Why is it advisable to allow telephone wires to sag between poles?​


Why is it advisable to allow telephone lines to sag when stringing them between pokes in summer? Because of the physical property of the materials used in cables that makes them dilate and contract with the changes of temperature. That means when winter comes, the cables will shrink.
