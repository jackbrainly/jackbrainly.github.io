11. 1. Compute for the soil moistures in dry mass basis (mcm) and volume basis (mcv) using the given values: FCv = 73% PWPv = 48% AMRm = 24% As = 1.2


pa brainliest po

To compute for the soil moistures in dry mass basis (mcm) and volume basis (mcv), we need to use the following formulas:

mcm = (Mw - Md) / Md x 100

mcv = (Mw - Md) / (Md x As) x 100

where:

Mw = moisture content on a wet basis

Md = moisture content on a dry basis

As = bulk density of soil (in g/cm^3)

Given:

FCv = 73%

PWPv = 48%

AMRm = 24%

As = 1.2

First, we need to convert the given values of FCv and PWPv to their corresponding moisture contents on a wet basis (Mw).

Mw_FC = FCv / 100 = 0.73

Mw_PWP = PWPv / 100 = 0.48

Next, we can compute for the moisture content on a dry basis (Md) using the AMRm.

Md = Mw / (1 + (AMRm / 100)) = 0.65

Now, we can use the formulas to compute for the soil moistures in dry mass basis (mcm) and volume basis (mcv).

mcm = (Mw - Md) / Md x 100 = (0.73 - 0.65) / 0.65 x 100 = 12.31 mcm

mcv = (Mw - Md) / (Md x As) x 100 = (0.73 - 0.65) / (0.65 x 1.2) x 100 = 10.26 mcv

Therefore, the soil moisture in dry mass basis (mcm) is 12.31 mcm and the soil moisture in volume basis (mcv) is 10.26 mcv.
