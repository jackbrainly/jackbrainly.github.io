1. Calculate the mole fraction of NaCl and H2O, if 0.010 moles of NaCl is dissolved in 5.56 moles of pure water


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

• For NaCl

[tex]\begin{aligned} X_{\text{NaCl}} & = \frac{n_{\text{NaCl}}}{n_{\text{NaCl}} + n_{\text{H}_2\text{O}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.010 mol}}{\text{0.010 mol + 5.56 mol}} \\ & = \boxed{0.0018} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• For H₂O

[tex]\begin{aligned} X_{\text{H}_2\text{O}} & = \frac{n_{\text{H}_2\text{O}}}{n_{\text{NaCl}} + n_{\text{H}_2\text{O}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{5.56 mol}}{\text{0.010 mol + 5.56 mol}} \\ & = \boxed{0.998} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the mole fractions of NaCl and H₂O are 0.0018 and 0.998, respectively.

3. Using of mole fraction calculate the mole fraction of alcohol C2H5OH and water in mixture of 9.2 grams of alcohol in 18 grams of water. ​


Solution:

First, we calculate the number of moles of C₂H₅OH and H₂O. The number of moles of C₂H₅OH is

[tex]n_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} = 9.20 \: g \: \text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH} \times \frac{1 \: mol \: \text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}}{46.068 \: g \: \text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} = 0.19970 \: mol[/tex]

and the number of moles of H₂O is

[tex]n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = 18.0 \: g \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O} \times \frac{1 \: mol \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O}}{18.016 \: g \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = 0.99911 \: mol[/tex]

Next, we calculate the total number of moles. We write

[tex]n_{T} = n_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} + n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{T} = 0.19970 \: mol + 0.99911 \: mol[/tex]

[tex]n_{T} = 1.19881 \: mol[/tex]

Finally, we can calculate the mole fractions of C₂H₅OH and H₂O. The mole fraction of C₂H₅OH is

[tex]X_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} = \frac{n_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}}}{n_{T}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} = \frac{0.19970 \: mol}{1.19881 \: mol}[/tex]

[tex]\blue{X_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} = 0.167}[/tex]

and the mole fraction of H₂O is

[tex]X_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = \frac{n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}}}{n_{T}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = \frac{0.99911 \: mol}{1.19881 \: mol}[/tex]

[tex]\blue{X_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = 0.833}[/tex]

5. Calculate the molarity, molality, and mole fraction of FeCl3 in a 25. 7% by mass solution. (Density = 1. 28 g/ml)​.


SOLUTION: (molality)

Step 1: Determine the mass of solute (FeCl₃) and the mass of solvent (water).

Assume that the mass of solution is 100 g. In this problem, 25.7% by mass means that there are 25.7 g of solute in 74.3 g of solvent.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \text{mass of solute} = \text{25.7 g} \\ & \text{mass of solvent} = \text{74.3 g = 0.0743 kg} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

The molar mass of FeCl₃ is 162.2 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of solute} & = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}} \\ & = \frac{\text{25.7 g}}{\text{162.2 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.15845 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molality of the solution.

The mass of solvent must be expressed in kilograms.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molality} & = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.15845 mol}}{\text{0.0743 kg}} \\ & = \text{2.13 mol/kg} \\ & = \boxed{2.13 \: m} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molality of the solution is 2.13 m.

------------------------------------------------------------

SOLUTION: (mole fraction)

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of solute and solvent.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & n_{\text{solvent}} = \text{74.3 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{18.02 g}} = \text{4.1232 mol} \\ & n_{\text{solute}} = \text{0.15845 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the mole fraction of solute.

[tex]\begin{aligned} X_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{n_{\text{solute}}}{n_{\text{solute}} + n_{\text{solvent}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.15845 mol}}{\text{0.15845 mol + 4.1232 mol}} \\ & = \boxed{0.0370} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the mole fraction of the solute is 0.0370

------------------------------------------------------------

SOLUTION: (molarity)

Step 1: Calculate the volume of solution in liters.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{volume of solution} & = \frac{\text{mass of solution}}{\text{density of solution}} \\ & = \frac{\text{100 g}}{\text{1.28 g/mL}} \\ & = \text{78.1 mL} \\ & = \text{0.0781 L} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the molarity of the solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molarity} & = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.15845 mol}}{\text{0.0781 L}} \\ & = \text{2.03 mol/L} \\ & = \boxed{2.03 \: M} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molarity of the solution is 2.03 M.

Given:

concentration = 36% by mass of aqueous HCl solution

solute: HCl

solvent: water

Required:

mole fraction

molality

Solution: (a)

Step 1: Determine the mass of the components present.

Since the given is in terms of percent by mass, we assume that the mass of the solution is 100 g. Therefore, 36% by mass aqueous HCl solution contains 36 g of HCl (solute) in 64 g of water (solvent).

mass of solute = 36 g

mass of solvent = 64 g

Step 2: Calculate the molar mass of each component.

For solute

molar mass of solute = (1.008 g/mol × 1) + (35.45 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solute = 36.458 g/mol

For solvent

molar mass of solvent = (1.008 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solvent = 18.016 g/mol

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of each component.

For solute

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{36 g}}{\text{36.458 g/mol}}[/tex]

moles of solute = 0.9874376 mol

For solvent

[tex]\text{moles of solvent} = \frac{\text{mass of solvent}}{\text{molar mass of solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solvent} = \frac{\text{64 g}}{\text{18.016 g/mol}}[/tex]

moles of solvent = 3.5523979 mol

Step 4: Calculate the mole fraction of solute.

[tex]\text{mole fraction} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{moles of solute + moles of solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mole fraction} = \frac{\text{0.9874376 mol}}{\text{0.9874376 mol + 3.5523979 mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mole fraction} = 0.22}[/tex]

Solution: (b)

Step 1: Calculate the molality of solution.

Since the moles of solute is already obtained in Step 3 of Solution (a), which is 0.9874376 mol, and the mass of solvent is already given, which is 64 g or 0.064 kg, the molality of solution is

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent (kg)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{0.9874376 mol}}{\text{0.064 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molality} = 15 \: m}[/tex]

14. Calculate the mole fraction of solute and solvent in a 30% aqueous sugar solution(C12H22O11)​


Strategy:

The mole fraction is a dimensionless quantity that expresses the ratio of the number of moles of one component to the number of moles of all components present. In general, the mole fraction of component [tex]i[/tex] in a mixture or solution is given by

[tex]X_{i} = \frac{n_{i}}{n_{\text{T}}}[/tex]

where [tex]n_{i}[/tex] and [tex]n_{\text{T}}[/tex] are the number of moles of component [tex]i[/tex] and the total number of moles present, respectively.

Pointers to Remember:

Mole fraction is dimensionless, meapning, it has no unit of measurement.Mole fraction is always less than one.The sum of mole fractions of all components is always equal to one.If the given quantity is mass, convert it to moles using the molar mass of the component of interest.If the given quantity is percent by mass, always assume that the mass of the solution is 100 g. Then, determine the mass of each component.

[tex]\\[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Determine the mass of the components present.

Since the given is in terms of percent by mass, we assume that the mass of the solution is 100 g. Therefore, 30% aqueous sugar solution contains 30 g of sugar (solute) in 70 g of water (solvent).

Step 2: Calculate the molar mass of each component.

For solute

[tex]\text{molar mass}_{solute} =[/tex](12.01 g/mol × 12) + (1.008 g/mol × 22) + (16.00 g/mol × 11)

[tex]\text{molar mass}_{solute} = \text{342.296 g/mol}[/tex]

For solvent

[tex]\text{molar mass}_{solvent} =[/tex](1.008 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

[tex]\text{molar mass}_{solvent} = \text{18.016 g/mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of each component.

For solute

[tex]n_{solute} = \frac{\text{mass}_{solute}}{\text{molar mass}_{solute}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{solute} = \frac{\text{30 g}}{\text{342.296 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{solute} = \text{0.0876434 mol}[/tex]

For solvent

[tex]n_{solvent} = \frac{\text{mass}_{solvent}}{\text{molar mass}_{solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{solvent} = \frac{\text{70 g}}{\text{18.016 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{solvent} = \text{3.8854352 mol}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the mole fraction of each component.

For solute

[tex]X_{solute} = \frac{n_{solute}}{n_{solute} \: + \: n_{solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{solute} = \frac{\text{0.0876434 mol}}{\text{0.0876434 mol + 3.8854352 mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{X_{solute} = 0.022}[/tex]

For solvent

[tex]X_{solvent} = \frac{n_{solvent}}{n_{solute} \: + \: n_{solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{solvent} = \frac{\text{3.8854352 mol}}{\text{0.0876434 mol + 3.8854352 mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{X_{solvent} = 0.98}[/tex]

SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & mass_{\text{ethanol}} = \text{46 g} \\ & MM_{\text{ethanol}} = \text{46.069 g/mol} \\ & mass_{\text{methanol}} = \text{64 g} \\ & MM_{\text{methanol}} = \text{32.042 g/mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of ethanol and methanol.

• For ethanol

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{ethanol}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{ethanol}}}{MM_{\text{ethanol}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{46 g}}{\text{46.069 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.9985 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• For methanol

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{methanol}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{methanol}}}{MM_{\text{methanol}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{64 g}}{\text{32.042 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{1.9974 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mole fraction of ethanol

[tex]\begin{aligned} X_{\text{ethanol}} & = \frac{n_{\text{ethanol}}}{n_{\text{methanol}} + n_{\text{ethanol}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.9985 mol}}{\text{1.9974 mol + 0.9985 mol}} \\ & = \boxed{0.333} \end{aligned}[/tex]
