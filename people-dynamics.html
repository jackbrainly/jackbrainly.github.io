Yes , Dynamics always express what a community can bring to the life of its members. We cannot ignore complexities, because dynamics influence people's thought, norms, traditions, and society every day in the world. The Community Dynamics data provides useful insight into how residents feel about where they live and can influence decisions about which facilities are provided in a specific neighborhood.

Community Dynamics are particularly fascinating when they bring contextual aspects to how people feel about where they reside. When combined with other datasets on homelessness, health, and jobs, we will begin to pose some intriguing questions about what variables can lead to residents' views about where they live. Similarly, question any of our beliefs of what constitutes a thriving community.

What is Community Dynamics?

The Community Dynamics data was created by Social Life, a non-profit organization that specializes in science and community programs that investigate how individuals are influenced by changes in the urban environment.

The Community Dynamics evidence provides useful insight into how residents feel about where they live and can influence decisions about which facilities are provided in specific neighborhoods.For example, if a neighborhood scores particularly low in terms of “Local social relationships,” it might be worthwhile to investigate what community associations, clubs, and events are actually active in that area.

The evidence from Community Dynamics highlights communities with a strong sense of identity, happiness, and local social relationships.

Comparing the Community Dynamics data to other contextual datasets may aid in identifying areas that are similar on a variety of socioeconomic indicators but vary significantly in terms of social relationships and expectations of the region. Examining this data side by side can assist you in selecting case studies of similar areas with different neighborhood complex results to investigate what other variables are at work that contribute to people of these similar areas feeling particularly isolated/included or positive/negative about their local area.

6. it is a social system composed of people structure and technology for achieving common goals in a dynamic environment​


Answer:

Organizations as Systems

Organizations exist and operate in a competitive environment, and can seem complex and highly confusing amorphous mess that is very had to comprehend and analyze. The notion of "Organization as Systems" refers to an approach to conceptualizing organizations as systems based on systems thinking and theory, in order to give clarity and perspective to study and analyze the organization. A system is defined by its boundaries - the limits that identify its components, processes, and interrelationships when it interfaces with another system.

Using systems overlay to understand organizations allows for the acknowledgement of the idea of systems composed of subsystems, their inter-relatedness and interdependence; the existence of boundaries that allow or prevent the interaction between various organization units and elements of other subsystems and environments. Systems theory likens the enterprise to an organism with interdependent parts, each with its own specific function and interrelated responsibilities. To ascertain certain subsystems requirements properly and to design appropriate organization's form (structure), functions, and sub-systems, etc., it is of primary importance to understand the purpose and mission of the system as a whole, and the relationship to its environment.

A number of various system views - logical system, open system, social system, operations system, performance management system, and control system - can be defined to aid analysis and improve the shared understanding of the organization. For example, the combined view of organizations as open, social systems is a systems theory approach to understanding organizations as organic, alive, dynamic and complex systems. Open systems, are essentially, goal-seeking, information feedback systems.
