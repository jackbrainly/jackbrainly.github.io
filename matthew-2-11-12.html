22. Matthew 4 1-11 reflection ​


Matthew 4:1-11 recounts the temptation of Jesus in the wilderness. This passage is significant because it shows us how Jesus overcame temptation and remained faithful to God's will.

In the beginning of the passage, Jesus fasts for forty days and nights in the wilderness, and Satan comes to tempt him. The first temptation is for Jesus to turn stones into bread to satisfy His hunger, but Jesus responds by quoting scripture: "Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that comes from the mouth of God."

The second temptation is for Jesus to jump off the pinnacle of the temple, with the devil challenging Him to test God's protection. However, Jesus again responds with scripture, saying "You shall not put the Lord your God to the test."

Finally, Satan offers Jesus all the kingdoms of the world in exchange for his worship. But Jesus rebukes him, saying "Be gone, Satan! For it is written, 'You shall worship the Lord your God and Him only shall you serve.'"

This passage teaches us the importance of staying true to God's will and relying on scripture to overcome temptation. Even though Jesus was hungry, tired, and facing extreme temptation, He remained faithful to God's word and resisted the devil's offers. As followers of Christ, we can learn from His example and remember that God's word is a powerful weapon against temptation.
