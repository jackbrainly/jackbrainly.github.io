THE LEAPING FROG: WORD PROBLEM

Problem:

Solve the word problem involving series and sequence. A frog wants to reach the end of a bamboo tree that has a height of a 78 meters. The frog leaps 4 meters during the day and slips 2 meters down during the night. How many days will it take the frog to reach the top of the bamboo?​

Answers with Step-by-step explanation:

In order to solve this word problem involving series and sequence, we can use our analytic and critical thinking skills to obtain the required data. We can also form a function in order to solve this puzzle. But we can also use a little bit of reverse engineering. As given in the problem, the frog leaps 4 meters during the day and slips 2 meters down during the night. This means that in a day, the frog can reach 2 meters. Since the top of the bamboo is 78 meters, this is the height that the frog needs to climb. The frog can keep doing this same routine for over 35 days.

On the 35th day, the frog has already leaped 70 meters.

On the 36th day, the frog has already leaped 72 meters.

On the 37th day, the frog has already leaped 74 meters.

On the daytime of 38th day, since the frog leaps constantly by 4 meters, he has already climbed the 78-meter tall bamboo. So the correct answer is 37 and a half days or we can just round this up to 38 days.

9. A bamboo pole is leaning against a tree. If the height of the tree is 12.2 meters and the angle made by the pole and the ground is 40Â°, what is length of the pole?


Answer:

18.98 meters, or approximately 19 meters

Step-by-step explanation:

The worded problem will fall under trigonometric solving worded problem.

In analysing the problem, if the height of the tree is 12.2 meters, and the length of the pole is not given but the angle of it with respect to the ground is 40°. Through imagination or sketching the problem, you can create right triangle. Since the tree is growing upward and the pole is leaning againts the tree, it will create 90°, 40° is given therefore 50° is the other one since 180° is the total angle of one triangle.

To solve the problem, apply SOACAHTOA, since angles and one length is given.

If you rotate the triangle, suppose 12.2 m is the base and the length of the pole is the hypotenuse of the triangle. You can come up with Cosu= Adjacent/Hypotenuse (u is the angle).

Cos40° = 12.2/h

Cos40° = 12.2/hh= 12.2/cos40

Cos40° = 12.2/hh= 12.2/cos40h= 18.98 meters, approximately 19 meters.

Therefore, the length of the pole is 18.98 or approximately 19 meters.

α = 40°

H = 12.2 meters

[tex]sin\alpha =\frac{H}{L}[/tex]

Find:

Length of pole, L

Solution:

[tex]sin\alpha =\frac{H}{L}[/tex]

28. A bamboo pole is leaning against the tree.If the height tree is 12.2m and the angle made by the pole and the ground is 40°,what is the length of the pole?


Answer:

The length of the pole is approximately 19 m.

Solution:

hypotenuse = [tex]\frac{opposite side}{sin 40degrees}[/tex]

hypotenuse = [tex]\frac{12.2 m}{sin 40}[/tex]

hypotenuse = [tex]\frac{12.2}{0.64}[/tex]

hypotenuse ≈ 19 m

Step-by-step explanation:

One of the given from the problem is the height the tree which is 12.2 m. This distance will serve as the opposite side of the angle of elevation of the bamboo pole from the ground. Another given was the angle of elevation which is 40°. Since the bamboo pole is leaning against the tree, the length of it will serve as the hypotenuse. We then use the formula for the sine function to derive the formula to find the hypotenuse. In doing so we have:

sin 40° = [tex]\frac{12.2 m}{hypotenuse}[/tex]hypotenuse (sin 40°) = 12.2 mhypotenuse = [tex]\frac{12.2 m}{sin 40}[/tex]hypotenuse = [tex]\frac{12.2}{0.64}[/tex]hypotenuse ≈ 19 m
