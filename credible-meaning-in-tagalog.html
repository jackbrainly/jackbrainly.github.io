11. One of the reasons why a learner needs to cite sources of information used in his/her research is to show his/her credibility as a writer. this means that the learner


A learner needs to cite sources of information used in his/her research to demonstrate his/her credibility as a writer that the learner uses reliable information in his/her research.

Detail of explanation:

Citations show your readers where you got your information, give them a way to critique your study based on the sources you utilized, and give them a chance to learn about earlier research on the topic of your study. Citing sources is also your best line of defense against plagiarism claims.

It's critical to reference other people's work because:Readers can find the sources you used if you properly cite your sources. Citations of sources aid readers in learning more about a subject. Reviewing footnotes or references from well-known sources ["citation tracking"] is one of the best methods for finding reliable, pertinent sources about a subject.Citing others' words and ideas shows that you have read widely on your subject and are, therefore, reporting your findings from an informed and critically engaged perspective. Your authorship of the work gains more credibility as a result of the list of sources you used.Your arguments can be supported by the theories of other researchers. In many instances, the arguments of another researcher might serve as the main background from which you can highlight the importance of your study and offer illustrative proof for how you responded to the "So What?" issue.Alternative approaches can be justified using the theories of other scholars. Your citations can be used as support for other points of view or the necessity to take different actions if you disagree with a researcher's conclusions or think there is a gap in their comprehension of the study subject. In the same way that other researchers' theories might strengthen your arguments, they can also damage your credibility if their study is refuted. When sources are properly mentioned, your reputation won't be harmed if the information they provide turns out to be false or incorrect. It keeps readers from assuming that you disregarded or ignored other people's conclusions, even if they are contested.Ideas are protected as intellectual property, and failing to credit the source of an idea can have severe consequences. In academia, failing an assignment or a course might result from failing to cite other people's intellectual property. In the business world, failing to properly credit other people's intellectual property can lead to legal action, ruining careers and reputations. When you cite sources as a college student, you'll develop the practice of appreciating and correctly citing other people's work.
