What are the given? B. PlanA. What trategy can you use to solve the problem? B.What is the formula in finding the percentage? D. Check.A. Check the answer? ​


Answer:

A. What is asked?

The percentage of tigers in the animal park.

B. What are the given?

125 total animals in the park

60% of the animals are lions and zebras.

B. Plan

A. subtraction

B. Formula: (part/whole) x 100%

D. Check

A. Add the percentage of lions and zebras (60%) and the percentage of tigers (40%) to make sure they add up to 100%.

Solution:

60% of 125 = 0.6 x 125 = 75

100% - 60% = 40%

Therefore, 40% of the animals in the park are tigers.

A. Understand:

The problem asks to find the percentage of tigers in an animal park that has 125 animals and where 60% of animals are lions and zebras.

B. Given:

Total number of animals = 125

60% of animals are lions and zebras

B. Plan:

A. We can first find the number of lions and zebras in the park by multiplying the total number of animals by 60%:

Number of lions and zebras = 125 x 0.60 = 75

B. Then we can find the number of tigers by subtracting the number of lions and zebras from the total number of animals:

Number of tigers = Total number of animals - Number of lions and zebras = 125 - 75 = 50

C. Finally, we can find the percentage of tigers by dividing the number of tigers by the total number of animals and multiplying by 100:

Percentage of tigers = (Number of tigers / Total number of animals) x 100%

C. Solution:

Number of lions and zebras = 125 x 0.60 = 75

Number of tigers = Total number of animals - Number of lions and zebras = 125 - 75 = 50

Percentage of tigers = (Number of tigers / Total number of animals) x 100% = (50 / 125) x 100% = 40%

Therefore, 40% of the animals in the animal park are tigers.

D. Check:

To check, we can verify if the sum of the percentage of lions, zebras and tigers adds up to 100%.

Percentage of lions = (Number of lions / Total number of animals) x 100% = (75 / 125) x 100% = 60%

Percentage of zebras = (Number of zebras / Total number of animals) x 100% = (75 / 125) x 100% = 60%

Percentage of tigers = (Number of tigers / Total number of animals) x 100% = (50 / 125) x 100% = 40%

60% + 60% + 40% = 160%

The sum of percentages is greater than 100%, which suggests an error.

However, we can see that the 60% includes both lions and zebras, so we are counting them twice, which is causing the sum to be greater than 100%. Therefore, the answer of 40% is correct.

27. similarities and difference of kingly lion and elderly lion​


Answer:

Here are some similarities and differences between a kingly lion and an elderly lion:

Similarities:

· Both are members of the same species (Panthera leo)

· Both have manes (although the mane of an elderly lion may be thinned or greyed)

· Both are apex predators and play important roles in their ecosystems

· Both are social animals and live in prides with other lions

Differences:

· A kingly lion is likely to be stronger and more physically fit than an elderly lion

· A kingly lion may have a fuller, darker mane compared to an elderly lion

· An elderly lion may have more difficulty hunting and competing for resources due to age-related physical decline

· An elderly lion may have a lower social status within the pride due to its advanced age and declining physical abilities.

Answer:

Similarities:

Both kingly lions and elderly lions are members of the Panthera leo species.They both have the signature mane that is characteristic of male lions.They are both apex predators, meaning that they sit at the top of the food chain.They are both social animals, living and hunting in groups known as prides.They both have an average lifespan of 10-14 years in the wild.

Differences:

Kingly lions are at the prime of their lives, while elderly lions are nearing the end.Kingly lions are at their largest and strongest, while elderly lions are beginning to weaken and shrink.Kingly lions are at the height of their reproductive powers, while elderly lions are no longer able to mate.Kingly lions are often the leaders of their pride, while elderly lions are more likely to be pushed to the periphery.Kingly lions have their whole lives ahead of them, while elderly lions are living on borrowed time.
