9. The ratio of nickels, dimes, and quarter in a jar is 3:4:7 respectively. If there are total of 112 coins in the jar. How many of them are dimes?


Answer:

N:D:Q = 3:4:7

112 = (3+4+7)x

112 = 14x

x = 8

multiply 8 by 3,4, and 7

3x = no. of nickels

3(8) = 24 nickels

4x = no. of dimes

4(8) = 32 dimes

7x = no. of quarters

7(8) = 56 quarters

24+32+56 = 112

Therefore, there are 32 dimes.

16. 5-6. You have the following coins in your pocket: 5 quarters, 6 dimes, 2 nickets and 12 pennies. What is the probability, as a decimal, you will draw a dime?​


Answer:

There are a total of 25 coins in your pocket. The odds of drawing a dime first, therefore, are 6 in 25, or 0.24.

Once you’ve drawn that dime, there are 24 coins remaining, of which 12 are pennies, so the odds of drawing a penny next are 12 in 24, or 0.5.

The overall odds of drawing a dime followed by a penny is the product of the two individual odds: 0.24 x 0.5 = 0.12

So your odds of drawing a dime and a penny, in that order, are 0.12
