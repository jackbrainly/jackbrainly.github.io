6 Classification of Drug

1. Inhalants — type of drugs that are easily accessible in the household. Inhalants are consists of harmful chemicals which are taken through inhalation. Most common inhalant in the country is the "Rugby".

Harmful effects: Using inhalants may cause a severe effects on the the nervous system. Permanent brain damage may occur and will resulted to impaired memory issue, uncontrolled movements, and if it's left uncontrolled then the person will shows a manifestation of addiction and withdrawal.

2. Stimulant — this is a type of drug that speeds up the function of the nervous system. In which the person may show a characteristics of being high and hyperactive. The most common stimulant in the country is the "Shabu".

Harmful effects: Consuming stimulants may cause an increase of heart rate, you might also feel alert and fully awake, and the most severe effects is that you are prone for anxiety and panic attacks.

3. Gateway Drugs— type of drug that can be a habit forming. In which the individual can consume more, harder, and may cause a sudden addiction. Most common drug is the Marijuana.

Harmful effects: May cause stress, sudden increase of heart rate and blood pressure. It can also cause a psychological dependence where the user may start in experimenting to take more potent type of drugs.

4. Depressant — type of drug which is the opposite of stimulant. It slows down the function of the nervous system.

Harmful effect: Since depressants are usually prescribed by a physician, overdose is the most common problem for those individual taking anti-depressants drugs. Consuming too much which is beyond what a person can handle may cause unconciousness, fatigue, and worse is death.

5. Hallucinogens — type of drug that can alter the person's perception of the reality.

Harmful effects: Hallucinogens can cause hallucinations in a form of sounds, images, and even through sesation. It can also trigger depression and panic attacks.

6. Narcotics — type of drugs that can alleviate pain and can induce sleep.

Harmful effects: Narcotics are usually a medicine with a high dosage and prescribed for people with mental disorders. Narcotic abuse can cause inflammations to the internal organs like lungs and liver. It can also cause psychological dependence to the drug.
