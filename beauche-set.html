Additional explanation:

What is the difference between a well-defined set and a not-well-defined set?


A well-defined set has parameters or conditions that are clear, distinct, and objective.

Examples:

All numbers that are divisible by fiveA set of real numbersAll the paintings made by Vincent Van GoghThe students in the classroom with the last name dela Cruz.The names of the female presidents of the Philippines

A not-well defined set has parameters or conditions that may not be clear to everyone. Its conditions may be subjective or may mean different from one person to another. It may also mean that the parameters or conditions provided are incomplete which prevents one from gathering all the data that fits the set.

Examples:

A set of happy people (not well-defined because happy may mean or look different for each person)A set of young people  (not well-defined because it does not say if young people include young adults or just children and teenagers)C = {feminine colors} (not well-defined since colors may be liked by either genders)Books that make people cry (not well-defined because any book can have a different effect on people)Sounds that scare babies (not well-defined because babies do not have the same reaction to sounds and not all babies can express fear the same way)

23. Toby skated from his house to the beach at a constant speed of 8 kilometers per hour, and then skated from the beach to the park at a constant speed of 7 kilometers per hour. The total distance Toby skated was 20 kilometers, and it took him twice as long to get to the park. Let b be the number of hours it took Toby to skate from his house to the beach, and p the number of hours it took him to skate from the beach to the park.


Answer:

8b+7p=20

p=2b

Step-by-step explanation:

1. We can represent this situation with two equations: an equation that represents the total distance Toby skated, and another equation that represents the proportion between the two durations.

2. Distance:

Toby skated to the beach at a speed of 8 kilometers per hour, and he did that for b hours, so 8b represents the distance Toby skated to get to the beach.

Similarly, Toby skated to the park at a speed of 7 kilometers per hour, and he did that for p hours, so 7p, represents the distance Toby skated to get to the park.

So the total distance is represented by 8b+7p and we need that to be equal to 20:

8b+7p=20

3. Duration:

The time it took Toby to get to the park is twice as much as the time it took him to get to the beach, so we need p to be twice as much as b:

p=2b

4. The Answer:

The system of equations that represents this situation is

8b+7p=20

p=2b

27. how to get the set on the universal set?​


Answer:

A universal set (usually denoted by U) is a set which has elements of all the related sets,without any repetition of elements. Say if A and B are two sets,such as A={1,2,3} and B={1,a,b,c}, then the universal set associated with these two sets is given by U={1,2,3,a,b,c}.
