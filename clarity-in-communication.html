6. why is there a need to consider appropriateness, brevity, clarity, ethics and vividness when engaging in verbal communication?


Answer

First, we define the words that need consideration

Appropriateness - the quality of being suitable or proper in the circumstances.Brevity - concise and exact use of words in writing or speech.Clarity - the quality of being coherent and intelligible.Ethics - moral principles that govern a person's behavior or the conducting of an activity.Vividness - producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind.

We consider these five qualities because we need to be sensitive, precise, understandable, logical, and clear in the message we are delivering. 24. clarity is any communicative act that must be fostered as it will facilitate better understanding of the intended messagetrue or false​


Answer:

To answer the question simply, this statement is false.

"Clarity is any communicative act that must be fostered as it will facilitate better understanding of the intended message."

The statement above is not correct. Clarity is the quality or characteristic of being understandable and coherent. It is not an act but it is the goal of good communication.

Instead of clarity, clarification is the right word to use. The statement is going to be true if it instead said: "Clarification is any communicative act that must be fostered as it will facilitate better understanding of the intended message."
