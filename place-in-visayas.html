Answer:

TACLOBAN CITY, Leyte, Sept 11 (PIA) -- The presence of active Philippine Fault zone and lineaments have put 29 municipalities in Eastern Visayas as high risk to earthquakes.

This was revealed by the Philippine Volcanology and Seismology (Philvocs) in the region where Myra Dolina, Philvocs science research analyst said that while recent movements of the fault lines have been recorded, the intensity were not really felt by residents because the intensity was lower.

Philvocs disclosed that the Central Leyte Fault forming part of the 1,200 kilometers Philippine Fault Zone stretches in some areas of Mahaplag, Ormoc City, Kananga, Capoocan, Javier, MacArthur, La Paz, Abuyog, Burauen, Leyte, and Albuera and where the crossing faults list as earthquake-prone areas the towns of Sogod, Libagon, Saint Bernard, Liloan, and San Ricardo in Southern Leyte.

The towns of Taft, San Julian, Sulat, Borongan City, Balangiga, and Lawaan in Eastern Samar as well as the towns of Marabut, Basey Pinabacdao and Hinabangan, Samar are subject to jolts by the movements of Southern Samar Lineament.

Philvocs also informed that the Northern Samar lineament traverses the towns of Allen, Victoria, and San Isidro.

It was learned that Philvocs is in the process of making hazard maps for Biliran close to the Philippine Fault Zone and some areas in Samar near the central Samar Lineament.

According to Philvocs, their office and some concerned government agencies have been disseminating the data to the local government units since 2007. (PIA-8)

25. 1. What are the characteristics of architectures in VISAYAS?2. How can architectures of different places in VISAYAS describe culture andhistory?* What is the importance of architectures of VISAYAS to the lives of people?​


Answer:

1, Visayans (Visayan: Mga Bisaya, local pronunciation: [bisaˈjaʔ]) or Visayan people, are a Philippine ethnolinguistic group native to the whole Visayas, the southernmost islands of Luzon and many parts of Mindanao. They are the largest ethnic group in the geographical division of the country when taken as a single group, numbering some 33.5 million. The Visayas broadly share a maritime culture with strong Roman Catholic traditions merged with cultural elements through centuries of interaction and inter-migrations mainly across the seas of Visayas, Sibuyan, Camotes, Bohol and Sulu and in some secluded areas merged with ancient animistic-polytheistic influences (i.e. Folk Catholicism). Most Visayans are speakers of one or more Bisayan languages, the most widely spoken being Cebuano, followed by Hiligaynon (Ilonggo) and Waray-Waray.
