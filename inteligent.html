4. 1 anu- ano ang 9 na inteligences ayo kay Howard garder?​


Answer:

1. Visual Spatial

2. Verbal/ Linguistic

3. Mathematical/Logical

4. Bodily/Kinesthetic

5. Musical or Rhythmic

6. Intrapersonal

7. Interpersonal

8. Existentialist

9. Naturalist

Answer(s):

1. Naturalist Intelligence - Naturalists have vast intelligence when it comes to nature. They are able to discriminate among living things, as well as sensitivity to other features of the natural world. They are usually deeply connected with nature (love for plants, animals, etc.)

2. Musical Intelligence - Musically intelligent people have the ability to greatly discern differences between tones, rhythms, pitches (and more). They are able to produce, recognize, or perform music more easily than others.

3. Logical-Mathematical Intelligence - Logically-mathematically intelligent people are able to calculate, conduct and consider hypotheses or theories, and complete/carry out mathematical equations or operations with less trouble. They are able to analyze, reason and solve problems more easily than others.

4. Existential Intelligence - People who are existentially intelligent are typically the ones able to see the big picture and understand people through intuition. They are also usually able to tackle deep questions of human existence.

5. Interpersonal Intelligence - Interpersonal intelligent people are able to effectively understand and communicate with others whether it's verbally, or non-verbally. They are more easily to track other people's moods, and see from other people's perspectives.

6. Bodily-kinesthetic intelligence - People who are kinesthetically intelligent are able to easily control their timing and power more so easily than others. They learn more by exploring, discovering and action.

7. Linguistic Intelligence - People who have linguistic intelligence are able to clearly understand written and spoken language. This goes the same for when they are expressing themselves by writing or speaking.

8. Intra-personal Intelligence - People who have intra-personal intelligence are able to understand their own thoughts and feelings. They are also knowledgeable when it comes it to planning their life and decisions.

9. Spatial Intelligence - People who have spatial intelligence are able to construct, transform and retain well-constructed visual images. They are also able to see the visual images clearly in various angles. They are able to think and see in three dimensions.

Harnessing the Power of KWL Charts in Education

KWL chart example

KWL Chart Template (Click on image to modify online)

What is a KWL chart?

A KWL chart is an organizational tool primarily used by students and teachers to direct and facilitate learning in the classroom.

K-W-L is an acronym that stands for “Know,” “Want to Know,” and “Learned.” The KWL chart is divided into three columns—one for each letter—under which students record:

What they already know about the topic

What they want to know (or questions they have) about the topic

What they learned (after the lesson or assignment)

KWL charts are effective tools for engaging students in the learning process, helping them recall knowledge, and tracking their learning progress. While they are often used to help students improve their reading comprehension, KWL charts can be applied to any topic or lesson.

Benefits of KWL charts in the classroom

KWL charts are a simple but powerful way for students to engage in the learning process. There are many reasons to try this visual tool in your classroom. KWL charts:

Are easy to use

Demonstrate the level of knowledge and gaps in understanding

Motivate and engage students in the learning process

Track progress and learning outcomes

Present a simple method for organizing notetaking

Offer flexibility and can be adapted to the lesson or student’s needs
