6. Why do artists stimulate volume in their art work? ​


ANSWER

An artist applies volume to artwork because it makes art look real and better by having an illusory weight.

EXPLANATION

Volume is the representation of mass in a 2D and 3D artwork. It creates the illusion that an object is occupying space. Volume tricks the mind by 'convincing' it that it is looking at a 3D shape that has mass, weight, and density. It is created by applying value to the lightness and darkness of an object. It mimics how light hitting object would create a bright area and shadows.

form- the external shape, appearance, or configuration of an object, in contradistinction to the matter of which it is composed; in Aristotelian metaphysics, the active, determining principle of a thing as distinguished from matter, the potential principle.

Mass-is both a property of a physical body and a measure of its resistance to acceleration (rate of change of velocity with respect to time) when a net force is applied. An object's mass also determines the strength of its gravitational attraction to other bodies. The SI base unit of mass is the

kilogram (kg).

Volume-is the quantity of three-dimensional space enclosed by a closed surface. For example, the space that a substance (solid, liquid, gas, or plasma) or 3D shape occupies or contains. Volume is often quantified numerically using the SI derived unit, the cubic metre

Texture-in painting refers to the look and feel of the canvas. ... The concept of "painterliness" also has bearing on texture. The texture stimulates two different senses: sight and touch. There are four types of texture in art: actual, simulated, abstract, and invented texture.
