5. Farm-to-market roads and post-harvest facilities are public investment in agriculture, why is it necessary to farming sector? ​


FARM-TO-MARKET ROADS AND POST-HARVEST FACILITIES ARE A PUBLIC INVESTMENT IN AGRICULTURE, WHY IS IT NECESSARY FOR THE FARMING SECTOR?

Farm-to-market roads and post-harvest facilities are necessary for the farming sector because it is a great help for the farmers or those whose livelihood is based on agriculture and fishing because this helps them to have easier access to their respective proper towns.

The farm-to-market roads connect the small towns and the rural to the market towns where farmers and fishermen could sell their produce and catch to a much better price. These farm-to-market roads are also concreted roads where small vehicles can fit, enough to carry the products of those from agricultural areas.

Furthermore, the post-harvest facilities also play a big factor in the fishing industry. One of the most known post-harvest facilities in the country is located in the places near the lower part of Luzon because of the abundant sardine in their area. These facilities are where the drying, bottling, and, canning takes place. Post-harvest facilities are also used for training to further enhance the skills of those who work in the facility and to provide much better quality products.
