Answer:

To find the answer of "10 - 2 + 6 ÷ 3", we need to use the method called GEMDAS.

GEMDAS has the same method as PEMDAS, except that the letter G on the word "GEMDAS" means Groupings, wherein it has:

- Parenthesis ("( )");

- Brackets ("[ ]"); and

- Curly Brackets ("{ }")

While the letter "P" on the word PEMDAS, means it is just Parenthesis.

Now, separating GEMDAS/PEMDAS into abbreviations:

G (For GEMDAS) - Groupings (or) P (For PEMDAS) - Parenthesis

E - Exponents

MD - Multiplication and Division

AS - Addition and Subtraction

So, moving on to the equation that is being asked on the question, here is the solution:

10 - 2 + 6 ÷ 3 = 10 - 2 + 2

Then, 10 - 2 + 2 = 8 + 2

Lastly, 8 + 2 ≈ 10

Therefore, the answer is 10.
