14. Red-green colorblind is:A.An X -linked traitB.A Y -linked traitC.Both X and Y linkedD.Neither of X and Y linked​


The answer is "A"

Red-green colorblindness: A form of colorblindness in which red and green are perceived as identical. This is the most common type of colorblindness. It is inherited in an X-linked recessive manner and affects 6% of males.

18. differentiate X-linked genes from Y-linked genes?​


differentiate X-linked genes from Y-linked genes?

A characteristic of X-linked inheritance is that fathers cannot pass X-linked traits to their sons (no male-to-male transmission). A condition is considered Y-linked if the altered gene that causes the disorder is located on the Y chromosome, one of the two sex chromosomes in each of a male's cells.
