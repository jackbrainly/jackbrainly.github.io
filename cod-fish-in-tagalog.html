9. Solve the given problem 100 students were randomly selected and it was found out that: 40 are playing ML 50 are playing COD 20 are playing both ML and COD 1. How many students neither play ML nor COD? 2. How many students prefer at least one of both games? 3. How many students play only one of ML nor COD?​


Sets and Venn DiagramAnswer30 students70 students50 students

Problem

The problem states the statistics of 100 random participants in terms of playing ML and/or COD. Here are the givens:

ML = 40COD = 50ML and COD = 20Total Participants = 100

One thing that could help us is to plot these given data using a Venn diagram. A Venn diagram is a visual illustration used to show the relationship between two or more groups of objects using overlapping circles. But also, along the way, we will solve this problem algebraically.

Plotting the data in a Venn diagram, the results are as follows.

n(ML) = 40n(COD) = 50n(ML ∩ COD) = 20   ...This means the intersection between ML and COD groupsn(U) = 100   ...The set U pertain to the "universal set" or the total number of objects present in all sets

To determine the number of students who play ML only, subtract the number of students playing ML by the number of students who play both ML and COD.

n(ML only) = n(ML) - n(ML ∩ COD) = 40 - 20 = 20

To determine the number of students who play COD only, subtract the number of students playing COD by the number of students who play both ML and COD.

n(COD only) = n(COD) - n(ML ∩ COD) = 50 - 20 = 30

Solution

Using the above information, let us answer the questions.

1. How many students neither plays ML nor COD?

n(not ML or COD) = n(U) - n(ML ∪ COD)

n(not ML or COD) = n(U) - [n(ML only) + n(COD only) + n(ML ∩ COD)]

n(not ML or COD) = 100 - [20 + 30 + 20]

n(not ML or COD) = 100 - 70

n(not ML or COD) = 30

∴ There are 30 students who neither plays ML nor COD.

2. How many students prefer at least one of both games?

n(ML ∪ COD) = n(ML only) + n(COD only) + n(ML ∩ COD)

n(ML ∪ COD) = 20 + 30 + 20

n(ML ∪ COD) = 70

∴ There are 70 students who play one or both games.

3. How many students play only one of ML nor COD?

n(ML only ∪ COD only) = n(ML only) + n(COD only)

n(ML only ∪ COD only) = 20 + 30

n(ML only ∪ COD only) = 50

∴ There are 50 students who only play one of the games.

Additional Notes

In the answer above, we used the symbols ∪ and ∩, which means union and intersection respectively. Now, union, as its name implies, pertains to the combination of two named sets or groups. For example, the union of students playing ML only and COD only [tex](ML\ only \cup COD\ only)[/tex]. On the other hand, the intersection means the common objects between the two groups. For example, the number of students playing both ML and COD is the intersection of the two groups [tex](ML \cap COD)[/tex].
