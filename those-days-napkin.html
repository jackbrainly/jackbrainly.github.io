28. A class is having a party for 20 students. They are buying cups in packages of 8 and napkins in packages of 12. What is the least number of packages of cups and napkins they should buy? a. 96 packages of cups and napkins b. 20 packages of cups and napkins c. 3 package of cups and 2 package of napkins d. 2 package of cups and 3 package of napkins​


Answer:

D. 2 packages of cups, and 3 packages of napkins.

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

8 - cups

12 - napkins

What to do:

Divide both given by 4.

[tex] \frac{8}{4} = \: 2 \: packages \: of \: cups[/tex]

[tex] \frac{12}{4} = 3 \: packages \: of \: napkins[/tex]

Answer:

So therefore, the number of packages of cups and napkins they should buy is 2 packages of cups and 3 packages of napkins.
