9. 3 apples and 2 melons cost 89.25 pesos. If a melon cost twice as much as apple, find the cost of one melon​


Answer:

P25.5

Step-by-step explanation:

The cost of a melon is twice as the apple. The total price is P89.25. 2 melons would be 4 apples then, having a total of 7. So the solution is:

P89.25 / 7 = P12.75

P12.75 * 2 = P24.5
