Answer and Step-by-step solution:

The cables of a horizontal suspension bridge are supported by two towers 40 ft high and 150 ft apart. If the bridge is tangent to the cables at its center, find the length of one of its vertical support 30 ft from one of the towers.

Write two equations to come up with a parabola with a minimum at the origin 0,0.

The bridge is the x-axis and the cable kisses the bridge at the origin

x=-75, y=40 and x=+75, y= 40

-75^2(a) - 75b = 40

5625a - 75b = 40

and

75^2(a) + 75b = 40

5625a + 75b = 40

Use elimination with these two equations

5625a - 75b = 40

5625a + 75b = 40

(Addition eliminates b, find a)

11250a + 0 = 80

a = 80/11250

a = .0071111

b cancels so we come up with a simple equation

y = .0071111x^2

"find the length of one of its vertical support 30 ft from one of the towers."

75 - 30 = 45 ft and

find y when x=45

y = 0071111(45^2)

y = 14.4 is the height of the cable at x = 45 ft
