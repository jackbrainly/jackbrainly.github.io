13. Which chords are major chords and minor chords ​


ANSWER

MAJOR CHORDS V.S MINOR CHORDS

•A major chord has what's called a “natural third.” It's the third degree of the chord's respective major scale.

•A minor chord has what's called either a “minor third” or a “flat third.” It's the third degree of the chord's respective minor scale.

Theorem: An angle between tangent and chord and an inscribed angle intercepted the same chord on opposite side are equal.

Given diagram, [tex]\angle CDE[/tex] is inscribed angle of Circle [tex]O[/tex] because an inscribed angle is the angle formed by points on the circle's circumference. Therefore, the measure of [tex]\angle CDE[/tex] is same as [tex]\angle ACE[/tex] which 40°.

For the [tex]\angle BCD[/tex] which in the diagram, it shows that the angle is between by the tangent and chord and an inscribed angle intercepted which are the same chord on opposite side are equal. In other words, [tex]\angle CED[/tex] and [tex]\angle BCD[/tex] has a same measurement which is 50°

Now, for [tex]\angle DCE[/tex], its measurement is the sum/difference of [tex]\angle CDE[/tex] and [tex]\angle CED[/tex].

[tex]\rm{\angle \:DCE+\angle \:CDE+\angle \:CED=180} \\\\ \rm{\angle \:DCE+40+50=180} \\\\ \rm{\angle \:DCE+90=180} \\\\ \therefore \rm{\angle \:DCE=90}[/tex]

Answer:

To put it simply, in a song, there's melody and there's chords. They do have some relation (certain notes do not sound good with certain chords, to various degrees) but they are two separate things.

In order to play the guitar you should know the chords (you already have that), in order to sing the melody you should find the notes sequence that consist it. That can be done my ear, or by reading a music score, like this example below. See that C in a square? That's the chord. The notes are the melody.
